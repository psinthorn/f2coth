## Purpose

F2 Co., Ltd. ("F2") requests the Client's permission to publish a
case study describing the professional relationship between the Client
and F2. The case study will appear on `https://f2.co.th/case-studies/`
and may be referenced in F2's sales and marketing materials.

## What we would display

If you sign below, F2 may display the following:

1. **Company name** — as it appears in official brand guidelines
2. **Company logo** — used per your brand-book guidance (please attach
   or link an approved asset pack)
3. **Industry label** and, optionally, property location
4. **A summary of the engagement** — challenge, solution, results — drafted
   by F2 and **submitted to the Client for written approval before publication**
5. **Optional quote** attributed to a named representative of the Client
   — only with a separate acknowledgement of the exact wording
6. **Optional hero image** — either supplied by the Client or captured
   by F2 with a location release
7. **Services provided by F2**

We will **not** display:
- Contract terms, pricing, or commercial figures
- Guest personal data, guest photographs, or reservation information
- Any information covered by NDA that pre-dates this consent

## Approval workflow

Before publication, F2 will send the Client a **preview link** with
the final rendered case study. The Client has **ten (10) business days**
to respond. Publication proceeds only after written approval by the
signatory below (or their designated successor).

## Legal basis — Thailand PDPA (พ.ร.บ.คุ้มครองข้อมูลส่วนบุคคล พ.ศ. ๒๕๖๒)

Where the case study identifies a natural person (for example the
quoted representative), that person consents under Section 24 of the
PDPA to the processing and public display of their name, title, and
quoted words for the purpose stated above.

## Withdrawal

The Client may withdraw this consent at any time by emailing
**dpo@f2.co.th** with the subject line "Withdraw case-study consent".
F2 will unpublish the case study within **five (5) business days**.
Cached copies on third-party search engines are outside F2's direct
control but will be requested for removal on a best-effort basis.
