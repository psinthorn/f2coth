## Scope of F2 engagement

F2's long-running engagement with SALA is focused specifically on **domain name registration and Domain Privacy management** across the entire portfolio. We are not SALA's general IT vendor — we are the trusted custodian of their domain estate.

## Challenge

SALA's 8 luxury properties operate as recognisable global brands. Each property has a primary brand site, plus sub-brand and protected name variants — country-specific TLDs (`.com`, `.co.th`, `.asia`), defensive registrations, and historical alternates accumulated over the decade.

**Key requirements:**

- **Unimpeachable hygiene** — an expired or DNS-misconfigured domain on an award-winning property is a brand incident, not a technical one.
- **Renewal discipline** — no missed deadlines across a portfolio that grows quarterly as new sub-brands launch.
- **Domain Privacy** — registrant details kept out of public Whois lookups to prevent spam and targeted social-engineering against SALA executives.
- **Consolidated billing** — one supplier, one currency (THB), one invoice line per renewal cycle.

## Solution

F2 manages SALA's complete domain portfolio under our **ResellerClub** partnership:

- **Registration & renewals** — every SALA-owned domain on the F2 management console, with auto-renewal enabled and human review before each cycle.
- **DNS hosting & configuration** — A / AAAA / CNAME / MX / TXT records managed centrally; same-day turnaround on routine changes.
- **Domain Privacy / WhoisGuard** — masking registrant details on every eligible name.
- **Transfers & defensive registrations** — handled as a service, including unlock codes, EPP keys, and registrar moves.
- **Consolidated billing** — one Thai Baht invoice covers the entire portfolio.

## Results

- **Zero domain-related incidents** in the 10+ year relationship.
- **100% of eligible names** under Domain Privacy.
- **Zero missed renewals.**
- **Single point of contact** for SALA's marketing and IT teams whenever a domain action is needed.
- **One PO covers the whole estate** — meaningfully simpler than managing eight property-level vendors.
